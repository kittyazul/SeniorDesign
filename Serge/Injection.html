CONTENT INJECTION


PART I: DEFINITION


Content injection is a technique used by attacker to insert malicious content into a legitimate site. By changing a part of the content on the page of a reliable website, users can be misled into entering personal information after being redirected to a page outside the legitimate website. Among other things, they may also be misled into downloading and installing malicious content containing ransomware, viruses, and more.





PART II: HOW ARE CONTENT INJECTION ATTACKS CONDUCTED?


Content Injection attacks are successful and effective when an application does not properly handle user-supplied data. That setback allows an attacker to easily supply malicious content to a web application, via a parameter value, that will then be reflected back to the user. As a result, the user is presented with a modified page under the context of the trusted domain. Generally, this attack is used in conjunction with social engineering because the attack is exploiting a code-based vulnerability and the userâ€™s trust.




PART III: WHAT CAN ATTACKERS DO AFTER SUCCESSFUL CONTENT INJECTION?


If successful, an attacker can easily mislead the user to enter their personal information. Even though the location bar will continue to display what appears to be a legitimate URL, the user will be using the generated or altered page made by the attacker. As a result, when the user enters sensitive data (such as a credit card number, password, bank account number, birth date, or Social Security number), the attacker will obtain that data for identity theft or some other fraudulent purpose.
