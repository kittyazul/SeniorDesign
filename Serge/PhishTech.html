PHISHING TECHNIQUES


UNDERSTANDING THE VARIOUS PHISHING TECHNIQUES


There are several different techniques that can be used to unethically obtain personal information from users. Throughout the years, as technology evolved, so did the cybercriminals' techniques.

To safeguard themselves against phishing attacks, users must have knowledge of how attackers think, what they use, and how they go about conducting their attacks. Therefore, they must be aware of anti-phishing techniques to protect themselves from becoming victims.

First, let's learn about the various phishing techniques that are available to attackers.



While regular phishing attacks rely on tricking unsuspecting victims into believing that the message is something they want or need, Spear phishing targets a specific individual or organization. The attacker will conduct extensive research on their target in order to make the attack more personalized and increase the likelihood of the target falling into their trap.

To learn more about Spear Phishing attacks, click here:   



In a session hijacking attack, an attacker exploits the web session control mechanism to steal information from the victim/user. By using a sniffer, the attacker is able to intercept relevant information so that they can access a Web server illegally.

To learn more about Session Hijacking attacks, click here:   



Content injection is a technique used by attacker to insert malicious content into a legitimate site. By changing a part of the content on the page of a reliable website, users can be misled into entering personal information after being redirected to a page outside the legitimate website. Among other things, they may also be misled into downloading and installing malicious content containing ransomware, viruses, and more.

To learn more about Content Injection attacks, click here:   



Link manipulation is a technique commonly used by attackers, in which they simply send a link to a malicious website. When the unsuspecting user clicks on the deceptive link, it opens the attacker’s website instead of the alleged legitimate website mentioned in the link. Simply hovering the mouse over the link to view the actual address can stop users from falling for this type of attack.

To learn more about Link Manipulation attacks, click here:   



This is the most commonly known and used phishing technique. In this attack, the same email gets sent to millions of users with a request to fill in personal information. Usually, that email will have an urgent note attached to it. This is intended to trigger an emotion of fear or panic in the victim, so that they feel pressured into taking immediate actions. The next steps will generally require the user to either enter their credentials to update account information, change their personal details, or simply verify their account.

To learn more about Email Spamming attacks, click here:   



Voice Phishing (commonly referred to as Vishing) is a phone phishing technique in which an attacker makes a phone call to a user and asks them to dial a number. The purpose is to get their personal information through the phone.

To learn more about Voice Phishing attacks, click here:   



Smishing is a phishing attack conducted via text message. It attempts to entice or trick a victim into revealing their personal information via a link sent by text, that will lead them to a phishing website.

To learn more about SMS Phishing attacks, click here:   



Phishing scams involving malware require it to be run on the user’s computer. The malware is usually attached to the email sent to the user or the downloadable file(s) within the email. Once the link is clicked, the malware will start functioning. Common malware used include, but are not limited to, Key loggers, Ransomware, Trojan, or Viruses.
